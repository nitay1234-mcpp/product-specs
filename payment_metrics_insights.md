# Payment Metrics Insights

## Summary

This document defines key metrics specific to the payment process, building upon checkout metrics insights. It provides a clear framework for measuring payment performance and identifying improvement opportunities.

## Metrics Definitions

### Payment Success Rate
- Definition: The percentage of successful payments out of all payment attempts.
- Calculation: (Number of successful payments / Total payment attempts) * 100
- Purpose: Measures reliability of the payment processing system.

### Payment Failure Rate
- Definition: The percentage of payment attempts that fail.
- Calculation: (Number of failed payments / Total payment attempts) * 100
- Purpose: Identifies issues affecting payment success.

### Payment Error Breakdown
- Definition: Categorization of payment failures by cause (e.g., user input errors, system errors, network issues).
- Purpose: Helps diagnose and prioritize fixes for payment problems.

### Average Payment Processing Time
- Definition: The average time taken to process a payment from submission to confirmation.
- Calculation: Total payment processing time / Number of payments processed
- Purpose: Indicates efficiency of the payment processing system.

### Discount Code Usage Rate
- Definition: The percentage of payments where a discount code is applied.
- Calculation: (Number of payments with discount code / Total payments) * 100
- Purpose: Measures effectiveness and adoption of promotional discounts.

### Repeat Payment Rate
- Definition: The percentage of users who successfully make multiple payments within a defined time period.
- Calculation: (Number of users with multiple payments / Total users) * 100
- Purpose: Indicates customer retention and satisfaction with the payment experience.

## Measurement Guidelines

- Collect data consistently across all payment platforms (web, mobile, app).
- Categorize payment errors for detailed analysis.
- Measure active processing time excluding idle periods.
- Integrate metrics into dashboards for real-time monitoring and alerting.

## Continuous Improvement

- Regularly review and update payment metrics definitions based on new insights and business needs.
- Conduct experiments and A/B testing to validate changes aimed at improving payment success and efficiency.
